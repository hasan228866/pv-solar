📚 Comprehensive System Explanation

📋 System Overview: This simulator models a configurable PV array connected to a first-order transfer function representing inverter/converter dynamics.

🔧 PV Array Model: Based on IEC 60904 standard with temperature and irradiance correction.
⚙️ Transfer Function: G(s) = b₀/(a₁s + a₀) represents system dynamics. Time constant τ = a₁/a₀ determines response speed.
📊 Output Analysis: System exhibits first-order step response characteristics with parameters like rise time, settling time, and steady-state values.
💡 Physical Interpretation: The transfer function acts as a low-pass filter, smoothing rapid changes in PV output. This represents combined effects of:
• DC-DC converter dynamics
• Inverter response time
• Output filter characteristics
• Control loop bandwidth

📖 How to Read the Charts

Voltage Chart (Blue): Shows exponential rise to steady-state. Time constant τ is time to reach 63.2% of final value.
Current Chart (Red): Usually responds faster than voltage. Rise time is from 10% to 90% of final value.
Power Chart (Green): Product of voltage and current. MPPT efficiency shows how close output is to PV maximum power point.
X-Axis (Time): Shows time in seconds. Total simulation time adjustable from 0.5 - 20 seconds.

📚 Terminology Glossary

MPP (Maximum Power Point): Operating point where solar panel produces maximum power.
Vmp: Voltage at maximum power point.
Imp: Current at maximum power point.
Voc: Open circuit voltage (no load).
Isc: Short circuit current.
STC (Standard Test Conditions): 1000W/m², 25°C, AM1.5.
τ (Tau): Time constant, time to reach 63.2% of final value.
Rise Time (Tr): Time from 10% to 90% of final value.
Settling Time (Ts): Time to reach and stay within 2% of final value.
